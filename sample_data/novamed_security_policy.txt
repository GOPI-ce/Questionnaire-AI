NovaMed Health — Information Security Policy
Version 3.1 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025

COMPANY OVERVIEW
NovaMed Health is a cloud-based SaaS platform that helps mid-size hospitals and clinics manage patient intake, medical record digitization, and HIPAA-compliant data sharing across care teams. Founded in 2018 and headquartered in Austin, TX, NovaMed serves over 340 healthcare organizations across the United States.

1. DATA ENCRYPTION
All data transmitted between NovaMed systems and client endpoints is encrypted using TLS 1.3 or higher. Data at rest is encrypted using AES-256. Encryption keys are managed via AWS KMS (Key Management Service) with automatic 90-day key rotation. Database backups are also encrypted at rest using AES-256.

2. ACCESS CONTROL
NovaMed enforces role-based access control (RBAC) across all system components. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all employee accounts and for customer admin accounts. Privileged access is granted on a least-privilege basis and reviewed quarterly. Access logs are retained for a minimum of 12 months.

3. VULNERABILITY MANAGEMENT
NovaMed conducts automated vulnerability scans weekly using Tenable.io. Critical and High severity findings must be remediated within 14 days and 30 days respectively. Penetration testing is performed annually by a qualified third-party vendor (most recent: October 2024, conducted by Bishop Fox). Application code is scanned using SAST tools (Semgrep) on every pull request.

4. INCIDENT RESPONSE
NovaMed maintains a documented Incident Response Plan (IRP). In the event of a data breach involving PHI (Protected Health Information), NovaMed will notify affected customers within 72 hours of detection, per HIPAA Breach Notification Rule requirements. A dedicated Security Incident Response Team (SIRT) is on-call 24/7.

5. BUSINESS CONTINUITY & DISASTER RECOVERY
NovaMed maintains a Recovery Time Objective (RTO) of 4 hours and a Recovery Point Objective (RPO) of 1 hour for Tier-1 services. Automated failover is configured across multiple AWS Availability Zones. Full DR drills are conducted bi-annually.

6. EMPLOYEE SECURITY TRAINING
All employees complete mandatory security awareness training upon hire and annually thereafter. Training is delivered via KnowBe4 platform. Phishing simulation exercises are run monthly. Employees who fail phishing simulations undergo additional targeted training within 5 business days.

7. PHYSICAL SECURITY
NovaMed does not operate its own data centers. All production infrastructure is hosted on AWS (us-east-1 and us-west-2 regions). AWS maintains SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, and FedRAMP certifications for its physical data center operations.

8. THIRD-PARTY RISK MANAGEMENT
All third-party vendors with access to NovaMed systems or PHI are required to sign a Business Associate Agreement (BAA). Vendor security assessments are conducted annually. Vendors are classified by risk tier (Critical, High, Medium, Low) based on data access and system integration level.

9. COMPLIANCE CERTIFICATIONS
NovaMed holds the following certifications as of 2025:
- HIPAA Compliance (self-attested and independently audited)
- SOC 2 Type II (last audit: August 2024, auditor: Prescient Assurance)
- HITRUST CSF Certification (certified March 2024)
- ISO 27001 (in progress, expected certification Q3 2025)

10. DATA RETENTION
Customer PHI is retained for the duration of the contract plus 7 years, per HIPAA requirements. Upon contract termination, NovaMed will securely delete or return all customer data within 60 days of written request. Deletion is performed using DoD 5220.22-M standards and a certificate of deletion is provided.
